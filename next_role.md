# What I'm Looking For in My Next Role

**What I want:**

I'm looking for a role where there's a direct line between my thinking and what ships. I've spent four years at a large e-commerce company owning strategy across search, navigation, B2B, and used goods — and I've learned that the part of product work I'm most energized by is the part that's hardest to do inside a big org: getting close enough to the product that your fingerprints are actually on it.

I want to work at a company where AI is core to the product, not a feature bolted on. I've been building with AI tools hands-on — LLM integrations, RAG pipelines, agentic workflows — and I want a role that rewards that fluency and pushes it further. Ideally that's an early-stage company with a live product, a real customer base, and a team that values PMs who prototype and build alongside engineers rather than just writing specs.

I'm also drawn to high-stakes domains — healthcare, care coordination, creative industries — where the product has genuine human consequence. That tends to attract teams with stronger mission alignment and raises the bar for how seriously everyone takes the work.

In terms of team dynamics, I work best with small, high-trust teams where ownership is clear and communication is direct. I want to grow into a product leadership role as the company scales, so I'm looking for a place where that path is real, not just implied.

**What I'd like to avoid:**

Large matrix organizations where design and engineering execution are so far removed from product strategy that the final product doesn't reflect the thinking behind it. I've been in that situation and it creates a ceiling on how much impact you can actually have — and how clearly that impact is visible.

I want to avoid companies that are "adding AI" to a legacy product as a reactive move. The best AI PM work right now is happening at companies where the product couldn't exist without the AI, not where AI is a dashboard feature being sprinkled in.

I'm also not looking for a pure roadmap management role. If I'm not close enough to the product to have opinions on interaction design, conversation flow, or technical architecture, I'm not in the right place.
