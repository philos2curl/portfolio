# Job Search Plan — Jerel Smith

---

## The Core Problem to Solve

The interview friction isn't your resume or your experience — it's the moment bhphoto.com comes up on screen and the interviewer forms an impression that doesn't match the work you actually did. Everything in this plan is built around two things: getting in front of the right people before that moment happens, and owning that moment when it does.

## Phase 1: Get the Foundation Right (Week 1)

### 1. LinkedIn — Treat It Like a Product

Your LinkedIn is probably the highest-leverage thing you're underinvesting in. Recruiters for the roles you're targeting will search LinkedIn before anywhere else.

**Update immediately:**
- **Headline:** "Senior PM | AI-Native Products | Search, B2B & E-commerce | Builder" — something that signals the AI builder angle prominently
- **About section:** Use the "describe yourself in a phrase" answer as the opener, then 3–4 sentences on your AI hands-on work, followed by the portfolio link
- **Featured section:** Pin your portfolio URL (philos2curl on Netlify) as the first item. This is the single most important thing — put it front and center before anyone asks
- **Experience:** Make sure B&H has two separate entries now (B2B & Used / Search & Navigation) matching your updated resume
- **Skills:** Add "Prompt Engineering," "LLM Integration," "Agentic AI," "RAG" — these are searchable and recruiters are actively filtering for them

### 2. Resume — One Tweak

Your resume is strong. The only thing to consider: add a one-line "Portfolio" entry near the top (below contact info) with your Netlify URL. Make it impossible to miss.

### 3. Prepare the bhphoto.com Answer

Write and rehearse a 60-second answer for: *"Walk me through bhphoto.com."*

The frame: "I own the strategy and product thinking — here's what that looks like in practice." Then pivot directly to the portfolio. Practice until it feels natural, not defensive.

## Phase 4: Activate Your Network (This Week)

This is almost certainly the highest-ROI thing you're underusing. Most senior PM roles are filled through referrals or warm outreach before they're ever posted.

### Who to Contact
Go through LinkedIn connections and identify:
- Former colleagues from HCA, Net32, VGT, TEGNA, B&H
- Anyone who has moved into a startup or AI company in the last 2 years
- Any PM or product leader you've interacted with professionally

### What to Say
Don't ask for a job. Ask for a conversation.

> "Hey [name] — I've been following what you're doing at [company] and it's exciting. I'm actively looking for my next PM role, focused on AI-native products and early-stage companies. Would you have 20 minutes to catch up? I'd love your take on the space and would appreciate any introductions if something comes to mind."

**Target: 10 outreach messages this week.** Even a 20% response rate gets you 2 conversations, which is 2 more than cold applications give you.

### LinkedIn Content (Optional but High-Value)
Post 1–2 times per week about what you're building or thinking. Something like:
- "I built [X] with Claude Code this week — here's what I learned about agentic UX"
- A take on AI product management that's grounded in your actual experience

This makes inbound happen. Recruiters will find you.

## Phase 5: Nail the Interview

### The Three Moments to Prepare For

**1. "Walk me through your current role."**
Lead with strategy and impact, not the org chart. End with: "I also want to show you something — I've built a portfolio that gives you a clearer picture of the thinking behind the work." Then share the link.

**2. "Pull up bhphoto.com."**
Have your answer ready: "What you're seeing is the design team's execution — my job is the strategy upstream of that. Here's an example of what that actually looks like:" → portfolio.

**3. "Show me something you built."**
This is where your portfolio wins. Walk them through the condition rating deck or the Used Hub concept. Show the artifact, explain the thinking, connect it to outcome.

### For AI PM Roles Specifically
Be ready to demo something live. If an interviewer asks "show me how you use AI tools," you should be able to pull up something you actually built. Keep one quick demo ready at all times.
