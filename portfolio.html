Mega Menu Navigation Redesign

B&H Photo · Search & Discovery · 2025–2026

↗ VP Deck ↗ Impact Dashboard
The Problem

B&H's navigation was a structural blocker to discovery. The existing mega menu wasn't merchandisable, made it difficult to surface new categories, and contributed to elevated bounce rates from shoppers who couldn't find what they were looking for. No PM had formally owned the navigation layer as a product surface.

I brought this to VP leadership as a revenue and retention problem, not a UX refresh.

What I Built & Projected
  • Executive deck built for VP-level approval, framing navigation as a discovery and conversion lever
  • Impact dashboard modeling projected lift across bounce rate, category entry, and session depth
  • Defined taxonomy, hover-state logic, and mobile behavior with engineering and UX scoping in parallel
  • Proposed A/B framework to measure discovery lift and downstream conversion post-launch

Used Condition Rating System Overhaul

B&H Photo · Used Department · Q1 2026

↗ Executive Deck ↗ PDP Prototype
The Problem

B&H used a legacy numeric grading scale (7–10) on the buy side and a completely separate 1–5 scale on the sell side. Industry competitors — MPB, KEH, Amazon, Adorama — had all moved to plain-language word-based scales. Customer verbatims in Medallia directly called out the confusion, with one reviewer describing the experience as "what a used car dealer would pull."

I presented the full case to the COO alongside the Director of the Used Department.

What I Built & Projected
  • 9-slide executive deck with competitive analysis, Medallia voice-of-customer data, and implementation roadmap
  • Proposed new 5-tier plain-language scale (Excellent → Rough) developed with the Used operations evaluator team
  • Fully interactive PDP prototype showing the new condition experience near the add-to-cart
  • Projected 8.5% CSAT improvement based on reduction in expectation-mismatch complaints

Sell Used — End-to-End Experience Redesign

B&H Photo · Used Department · 2025–2026

↗ Problem Analysis ↗ Traffic YTD ↗ Upload Prototype
The Problem

The Sell Used funnel had significant friction: unclear value proposition at entry, a confusing quote flow, no image upload capability for sellers to document their item's condition, and the same scale fragmentation problem that hurt buyer confidence.

I audited the full funnel, mapped where sellers dropped off, and built a prioritized problem backlog backed by traffic data.

What I Built & Projected

  • YTD 2026 traffic analysis surfacing where seller intent was high but conversion was low
  • Working image upload prototype — sellers can document item condition before submission, reducing appraisal surprises and building trust in the quote process
  • Tied directly to CSAT improvement initiative targeting the "felt scammy" Medallia feedback category

K-12 School Safety Landing Page

B&H Photo · B2B Hub · 2025

↗ v1 Classic ↗ v2 Explorer ↗ v3 Campus
The Problem

The existing K-12 page on the B2B Hub showed only cooperative contracts and generic non-clickable categories — no coherent story, no trust signals, no lead capture. Stakeholders wanted a dedicated school safety destination that could earn credibility with administrators, surface B&H's five areas of focus, and funnel prospects to an account manager.

I wrote the brief, defined the content architecture, and produced three distinct low-fi wireframe directions for stakeholder review.

What I Built & Projected
  • Three wireframe variants exploring different narrative structures: Classic Scroll, Interactive Explorer, and Campus-first layout
  • Five content pillars defined: Physical Security, Emergency Comms, Video Surveillance, Cybersecurity, and Student Wellness
  • Lead capture funnel designed to route prospects to account managers by territory
  • Compliance resource layer (Alyssa's Law, state requirements) to position B&H as a trusted K-12 authority, not just a vendor
UX Brief Figma B2B · EDU

EDU Advantage Landing Page Redesign

B&H Photo · EDU Program · 2025

↗ Figma Design
The Problem

FullStory and GA data showed the EDU landing page was generating a high volume of dead clicks — users clicking non-interactive elements expecting navigation that wasn't there. Benefits were buried, the sign-up flow lacked clarity, and Instagram content at the bottom of the page was auto-populated from the global feed, giving the EDU team no ability to control their own narrative.

I identified the dead-click pattern, wrote the redesign brief, and drove the Figma design through to a dev-ready handoff.

What I Built & Projected
  • Redesigned benefit hierarchy to lead with student value — savings, selection, shipping, expert help — before the sign-up ask
  • Clarified the 4-step verification flow (Verify → Confirm → Create → Explore) with visible progress cues
  • Proposed URL-specified Instagram embed replacing auto-feed, giving EDU control over curated content
  • Eliminated all dead-click elements; every interactive region mapped to a clear destination or action
Working Prototype B2B · Infrastructure

B2B CMS — Proof of Concept

B&H Photo · B2B Content Operations · 2025–2026

Coming Soon
The Problem

B&H has long-standing organizational hesitation around committing to a CMS — the build vs. buy tension is real, and the business has historically defaulted to static pages and manual publishing workflows. B2B content in particular suffers from this: landing pages, hub pages, and program pages require engineering involvement for every edit.

I built a functional CMS proof of concept to demonstrate what editor-controlled, structured content could look like — lowering the cost of the argument by making it tangible.

What I Built & Projected
  • Functional prototype showing a structured content editing interface mapped to real B2B page templates
  • Demonstrated that a lightweight CMS layer could give non-technical stakeholders control over EDU, K-12, and hub page content without engineering tickets
  • Built as a decision-making artifact, not a pitch deck — the goal was to remove "we don't know what it would feel like" as a blocker to commitment
