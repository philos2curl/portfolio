# Why Premise Interests Me

The "Figma for Hollywood" framing is exactly the kind of problem I'm drawn to. I've spent years working adjacent to creative collaboration — using Figma for my own wireframes and handoffs, and building a CMS proof of concept at B&H because I could see how much friction non-technical creative teams had with content ownership. Premise is solving that same problem at a much higher-stakes level, for an audience that lives and dies by the quality of the story.

I've also done 0→1 work inside a constraint-heavy organization. The CMS POC, the Used Hub concept, the condition rating overhaul — none of these were greenfield. Each one required making a case for something that didn't exist yet to people with strong opinions. I'd argue that's actually harder than 0→1 at a startup where everyone is already bought in. I know how to build conviction before there's a product to point to.

My gaming background gives me a genuine thread into entertainment. As a Product Owner at Video Game Technologies, I was working in a space where narrative complexity, creative vision, and production timelines all collided. Screenwriters and game writers deal with the same fundamental problem: managing a living story across multiple collaborators without losing the thread.

What drew me to this specific role is that I understand the gap between what a PM contributes and what the world can see. I built my entire portfolio around closing that gap — showing the thinking behind the work, not just the outcome. That's what Premise needs at 0→1: someone who can define what good looks like before anything ships, and bring the founders, creatives, and partners along with them.
