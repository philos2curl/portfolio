01 · Problems to Solve
Why this matters

Problems to Solve

📉

Order vs. Engagement Mismatch

Featured image slots don't reflect actual customer engagement rankings. In Lighting, Fluorescent Lights holds slot 3 but ranks #17 in engagement — prime real estate wasted on low-interest categories.

e.g. Fluorescent Lights: slot 3, rank 17
👁️

Non-Standard Scanning Pattern

The current mega menu layout does not adhere to F or Z scanning patterns. After the top-4 featured items, the list renders in a multi-column grid that forces non-linear reading — creating measurable friction for customers.

Category landing pages DO use Z-pattern
🌳

Deep Taxonomy Inaccessibility

The category tree reaches 7 levels deep. The average browsing path requires 3–4 clicks just to reach a meaningful sub-category. The worst-case scenario requires 7–8 interactions with very little value along the way.

Avg. depth: 4 · Max: 7 levels
🎛️

No Merchandising Control

Merchandisers have no mechanism to promote seasonal, new, or strategically important categories into featured slots. All category changes require developer tickets with no business-logic layer in between.

🔍

Search Doesn't Substitute

Highly specific categories like "Professional Video Degaussers Accessories" return only 1 search result. The curated taxonomy tree is irreplaceable for surfacing this breadth — but customers can't efficiently navigate to it.

1 result for "Prof. Video Degaussers Acc."
📱

Screen Height Blind Spot

On screens under 800px, users see only the top 4 featured items plus "See More." The high engagement rate on "See More" across multiple categories signals customers aren't finding what they want in the featured slots shown.

Mobile: 56.7% drop-off at step 1

02 · Product Objectives
What success looks like

Product Objectives

Six capability goals that together transform the mega menu from a static navigation element into a dynamic, merchandisable surface.

01

Merchandising Showcase

Empower merchandisers to promote new, seasonal, or strategically important categories into featured slots — without developer involvement.

02

Maximum Category Depth

Let customers access L3/L4/L5 sub-categories directly from the mega menu flyout — eliminating 2–4 intermediate clicks from the average browsing path.

03

Engagement-Driven Order

Slot assignments should reflect actual customer engagement data. High-engagement categories surface first; low-engagement, low-business-focus categories move down.

04

Customer Guidance Funnels

Lead customers into expert-driven journeys for popular use cases (e.g. Podcasting by Application). The menu becomes an entry point to curated experiences, not just links.

05

Promote Savings Opportunities

Surface contextual Specials and seasonal campaign links within the menu flyout — presented in the right category context at the moment of browsing intent.

06

Dynamic Learning Opportunities

Enrich the customer journey with contextual Explora content — buying guides, hands-on reviews, and video tutorials — surfaced inline within the relevant category flyout.

04 · A/B Test Plan
Launch Approach

A/B Test Plan

CONTROL Current State

  • Existing mega menu with static, developer-set category order
  • No L3/L4 depth promotion — customers click through intermediate pages
  • Subpar Explora and specials experience in flyout
  • Non-F/Z scanning pattern in sub-category list

TREATMENT New Design

  • Merchandiser-configured slot order based on engagement data
  • L3/L4 categories directly accessible from mega menu flyout
  • Shop & Learn column: Specials, Used, New + Explora content
  • Improved scanning pattern with sectioned column layout
Statistical Rigor

Measurement Standards

Methodology

Random session-level split: portion of visitors served new layout simultaneously. Same season, campaigns, and traffic mix for both groups — causal attribution for placement changes.

Significance Threshold

Don't act on lift until it clears 95% confidence. Runtime determined before looking at results to avoid early stopping. High-traffic slots reach significance faster; long-tail categories need longer windows.

Baseline Capture

2–4 week clean baseline window (matched day-of-week, no holiday distortion) captured for all Tier 1–4 KPIs on affected slots before any change goes live.

Novelty Effect Guard

New placement often spikes then settles as repeat visitors adapt. Let results stabilize before reading steady state. Segment by new vs. returning, device, and logged-in vs. guest — aggregate can hide both a win and a loss.

KPI Framework

Success Metrics

A placement change should show effects cascading top to bottom. Menu engagement moves first; revenue confirms. Always lead with rates over counts.

Tier 4

Revenue & Value

Attributed RevenueOrder value attributable to menu-sourced sessions per category
Revenue per Impression (RPI)Real estate productivity — the key cross-slot comparator
Revenue per Click (RPC)Revenue efficiency per click — signals quality of demand, not just volume
Average Order Value (AOV)Basket size of menu-sourced orders; margin-weight where available
Decision Rule

A placement change is a success when — after reaching significance — it produces a positive net Δrevenue and the promoted slot's RPI meets or beats the slot it displaced, without degrading conversion quality. Anything else is a reshuffle or a revert.

05 · Net Impact & Reporting Cadence
Measuring the full picture

Net Impact & Reporting

📊 The Net Impact Formula

Every promotion forces a demotion. Gains and losses must both be measured. We never declare a win on the promoted category alone.

Net Δrev = +Δrev (promoted)
+ Δrev (demoted) // usually negative
+ Δrev (adjacent slots)

Three outcomes: Net positive → keep. Reshuffle → cosmetic, not productive. Net negative → revert.

🔍 Demotion Intelligence

A demoted category whose traffic barely moves is the best candidate to demote — it frees prime real estate at minimal cost. Demotion analysis is how merchandising finds these candidates.

Watch for resilience: if demoted category traffic holds via search or direct, it was over-merchandised. Watch for collapse: if its revenue drops more than the promoted category gained, the trade is net-negative.

⚠️ Cannibalization Watch

If total department sales are flat but the promoted sub-category grew, the gain mostly cannibalized sibling categories rather than creating incremental demand.

Always measure total category-level demand alongside individual slot performance to distinguish real growth from internal redistribution.

Cadence Audience Content
Real-time / Daily Merchandising operators Slot CTR, impressions, anomaly flags — catch broken or mis-performing placements fast
Per-change readout Merchandising + Product Full Tier 1–4 cascade + net-impact verdict for each slot swap, once statistically significant
